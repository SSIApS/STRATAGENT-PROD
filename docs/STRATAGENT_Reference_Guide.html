Intelligence Depth
Knowledge Base scoring system — 0 to 100

Intelligence Depth is the score that measures how much actionable sales intelligence has been captured for a supplier in the Knowledge Base. It is not a simple word count — it rewards specific, verifiable content with domain vocabulary and measurable data points.

Thresholds

INTELLIGENCE GAP — <50 VALUE BRIEF READY — 50+ PROPOSAL READY — 80+ SINGULARITY READY — 90+
Field Intelligence requires a minimum Intelligence Depth of 50 to run. Below 50, the supplier profile is too thin to research prospects credibly.

Scoring Elements & Weights

ElementKeyMax WeightWhat earns points
Product Catalogue product_catalogue 20 pts Product series, models, variants, formats, capacities
Technical Datasheets technical_datasheets 15 pts Temperatures, pressures, voltages, tolerances, IP ratings, dimensions
Case Studies case_studies 20 pts Named customers, projects, challenges solved, measurable outcomes
Distribution Channels distribution_channels 12 pts Retailers, resellers, private label use, volume pricing, channel names
Certifications certifications 10 pts ISO, ATEX, CE, DNV, UL, FDA, REACH, RoHS, IECEx, NORSOK, API
Competitive Positioning competitive_positioning 10 pts Named competitors, win/loss context, differentiators, market position
Reference Projects reference_projects 10 pts Named sites, plant installations, commissioned projects, country/region
Pricing Framework pricing_framework 8 pts Price points, MOQ, volume tiers, currency, contract structures
Objections & Responses objections_responses 5 pts FAQs, warranty terms, lead times, common sales objections
TOTAL 100 pts

Quality Scoring Formula (per element)

Each element gets a quality ratio (0.0–1.0) multiplied by its max weight. The ratio is built from four components:

quality_ratio = presence_tier + depth_bonus + key_term_score + specificity_score

presence_tier   = 0.15 (text <30 chars) → 0.30 → 0.45 → 0.50 (text >300 chars)
depth_bonus     = min(0.20, paragraph_count × 0.04)      — rewards accumulated sources
key_term_score  = min(0.15, matching_domain_terms × 0.025)
specificity     = min(0.15, regex_hits × 0.03)            — numbers, codes, measurements

element_score   = quality_ratio × max_weight

Specificity patterns that add points: physical units (°C, bar, V, kW, mm, kg), certification codes (ISO 9001, ATEX II), model/part numbers, years, currencies (EUR, DKK, USD), decimal measurements.

Convergence Index
Prospect alignment score — 0 to 100

The Convergence Index (CI) measures how well a specific prospect's world aligns with a supplier's capability. It is scored by Gemini during Field Intelligence research, incorporating both static intelligence (KB content) and live buying signals found during research.

Output Paths by CI Score

PARK — <60 FIRST SIGNAL — 60+ MUTUAL VALUE BRIEF — 75+ CONVERGENCE PROPOSAL — 90+

What raises the CI

FactorImpact
Verified buying signal (TENDER, CAPEX)+10 to +20 pts vs no signals
Identified decision maker with LinkedIn+5 to +10 pts
Named operational facility or site match+5 to +15 pts
Active project in relevant product category+10 to +20 pts
Regulatory pressure creating obligation+10 pts
Matching certifications (supplier ↔ buyer requirement)+5 to +10 pts

Honest Gate

When CI < 60, STRATAGENT auto-parks the opportunity and returns an Honest Gate message. It also queries Gemini to find 3 alternative prospects with higher estimated CI, and saves the parked position to Active Watch with a threshold trigger at CI 60.

The CI is intentionally hard to inflate. A score of 62 means "approach is possible." A score of 91 means "approach now — the window is open."
Buying Signals
Live prospect events that create purchasing windows

Buying Signals are concrete, time-stamped events found during Field Intelligence research that indicate a prospect is likely to be in a purchasing window. Each signal has a type, strength rating, timing, and source. They are surfaced before the Relationship Profile in the results view and directly influence the Convergence Index.

Signal Types

TENDER
Published tender notice or awarded contract in this product category. Highest-value signal — procurement cycle is live.
LEADERSHIP_CHANGE
New CPO, Head of Procurement, Plant Manager, Sustainability Director. New leaders review suppliers in first 90 days.
CAPEX
Capital expenditure announcement — new facility, expansion, equipment investment. Creates a procurement event.
BUDGET
Approved budget disclosure, fiscal year spending plan, investment round. Indicates funds are allocated.
SUSTAINABILITY
Decarbonisation commitment, ESG pledge, technology upgrade program. Drives procurement of compliant products.
STRATEGIC_SHIFT
M&A activity, new market entry, operational restructuring. Changes the supply chain picture.
NEWS_EVENT
Regulatory change, supply chain disruption, certification requirement. Creates indirect demand.
SOCIAL_SIGNAL
Reddit threads, X posts, blog posts, or reviews where operators complain about the exact problem the supplier solves. Earliest-stage signal — pain before procurement intent has formed. A thread of 50 Airbnb hosts complaining about the same problem is a market signal, not just one lead.

Signal Strength

HIGH — Verified, sourced, directly relevant MEDIUM — Credible but indirect or inferred LOW — Weak or unverified indication

Approach Window

Synthesised from all active signals, the Approach Window describes the optimal moment to make contact — e.g. "Before Q2 budget freeze" or "While new CPO is still onboarding." If no window is identifiable, this field returns null and is not shown.

CI Impact Rule

A verified HIGH-strength signal (TENDER or CAPEX) should add 10–20 points to the Convergence Index compared to an equivalent prospect with no signals. This is the logic that converts a 62 CI to a 91.
Knowledge Base
Module 1 — Supplier intelligence repository

The Knowledge Base (KB) stores structured sales intelligence for each supplier Jason represents. Each supplier gets their own KB document in Firestore. The KB is the foundation that all other modules depend on.

KB Creation Flow

1. Enter company name + optional website URL → Gemini web research runs (30s) → structured profile created → Intelligence Depth scored → KB saved to Firestore.

2. After creation: enrich by adding PDFs via Upload Document, URLs via Add Web Source, or running STRATALYST.

Gap List

Elements below their score potential appear in the Gap List. Clicking a gap item scrolls to the Add Web Source panel, pre-fills a context note, and highlights the panel with a gold border — ready to receive the URL that will fill that gap.

Add Web Source

Fetches a URL, extracts structured intelligence from the page content, merges it into the existing profile, and rescores. Pass a focus element (which gap you're filling) and an optional context note to give Gemini a prioritisation hint.

Product Images

Images are uploaded per-supplier, tagged with product_name, brand, and comma-separated tags. Stored as base64 in the product_images Firestore collection. Searchable via GET /knowledge-base/{id}/images/search?q=. Used by the Output Engine to embed images in proposals. Max 5 MB per image.

Folder Sync

See Folder Sync Rules. Once synced, subfolders are routed to specific KB elements: Case Studies/ → case_studies, Product/ → product_catalogue, Certifications/ → certifications, Campaigns/ → competitive_positioning, Markets/ → operational_context.

Editable Website URL

The website URL on a KB can be edited directly in the supplier view — click the ✎ pencil icon next to the URL, type the correction, and press Enter or Save. Works on all KBs including those with no URL set yet. Endpoint: PATCH /knowledge-base/{id}/update-url.

Multi-Product Suppliers (STRATATIV3D pattern)

When a KB represents Jason's own product line rather than a third-party supplier, it operates at two layers:

Layer 1 — KB Profile (brand layer)
The 9-element profile covers the brand as a whole — manufacturing capability, positioning, certifications, buyer profiles. This is the sales pitch for the brand, not any individual product. Example: STRATATIV3D's KB profile describes its 3D printing / additive manufacturing capability and the type of buyer it appeals to.
Layer 2 — Product Library (product layer)
Individual products live in the Product Images section, each tagged with product_name, brand, and keyword tags. Example: Ungunk is registered as product_name: Ungunk, brand: STRATATIV3D, tags: charger guard, bathroom, airbnb, electric toothbrush, monthly replacement. The Output Engine can query by tag to pull the right product into a proposal.
All KB suppliers use the same 9-element structure. A multi-product supplier does not have more categories — it may appear to have more gaps because the brand site is minimal or pre-launch, leaving most elements unfilled.

Product Sub-KB (planned evolution)

As STRATATIV3D's product line grows, the correct long-term structure is a product sub-KB per product — each product (Ungunk, future products) gets its own KB with its own Intelligence Depth score, pricing framework, case studies, and buyer profile. STRATATIV3D becomes the parent brand; products are children.

This is not yet built. Until it is: KB profile = brand intelligence, Product Library = individual product entries.

Field Intelligence
Module 2 — Prospect research and Convergence Index

Field Intelligence researches a prospect company against a selected supplier's KB. It produces a Relationship Profile, a Convergence Index score, and a set of Buying Signals — then recommends an output path or auto-parks the opportunity.

Prerequisites

The selected supplier must have an Intelligence Depth of 50 or above. Below 50, the button is disabled and a warning is shown.

Research Flow

1. Select supplier → enter prospect company name → Run Field Intelligence → Gemini web research (grounded) → Relationship Profile + Buying Signals returned → CI scored → result displayed.

2. If CI ≥ 60: contextual output banner appears inline. If CI < 60: Honest Gate + 3 better alternatives + Park in Watch option.

Result persistence: The most recent result per supplier is restored when you navigate back to FI. "+ New Research" button clears it to start fresh.

Signal Recency — CRITICAL DESIGN PRINCIPLE

The research agent knows today's date. Signals older than 18 months are excluded from active buying signals and moved to the Historical Record. An approach window that has already closed is cleared entirely. The Convergence Index only reflects current intelligence — a 2023 acquisition does not drive a 2026 contact recommendation.
Signal ageTreatmentDrives CI?
Under 18 monthsActive Buying Signal — displayed in full colourYes
Over 18 monthsHistorical Record — preserved as context, greyed out, collapsedNo
No date foundTreated as recent (included)Yes

Historical Record Panel

Collapsed section in FI results labelled "Historical Record — N past signals." Expand to see all signals older than 18 months. Each shows type badge, timing, and signal description at 60% opacity. Useful for understanding the company's story over time — e.g. an acquisition two years ago explains current capacity and growth trajectory. Never drives an Approach Window or CI score.

Inline Output Generation

When CI ≥ 60, a gold action banner appears directly below the Convergence Index dial — no navigation to the Output Engine required. Label and content adapt to CI:

CI rangeBanner labelGenerated documents

90+CONVERGENCE PROPOSAL readyEmail + full technical proposal + engagement brief / RFQ framework

All outputs render inline as select-all text blocks (copy straight out). Regenerate button for a second pass. SSI contact footer appended to all client-facing documents automatically.

Buyer Type Awareness

The research agent is instructed that industrial insulation contractors and installers are direct buyers of insulation materials — not intermediaries. Companies described as "entreprenørfirma," "installatør," or "insulation contractor" receive a minimum CI of 65–80 when researched against an insulation supplier. This prevents the agent from penalising alignment for the most important buyer type in the industrial insulation market.

Park Logic

When CI < 60, an Honest Gate message is shown with 3 better-matched alternatives. A "Park in Watch" button saves the prospect to Active Watch with a threshold trigger at CI 60. No auto-park — Jason decides.

Active Watch
Module 3 — Parked opportunities with trigger conditions

Active Watch stores prospects that are not yet ready for an approach. Each monitored position has a trigger condition — when met, the position surfaces for re-evaluation.

Trigger Types

TypeFires when
thresholdCI reaches a specified score (default: 60)
timeA date is reached — e.g. next fiscal year, post-announcement
documentA specific document or tender is published
eventA named event occurs — leadership change, project award

STRATADAR Integration

Active Watch is now operated by STRATADAR — the cross-supplier intelligence monitor. The Active Watch page shows a STRATADAR dashboard with priority-scored positions, urgency labels, age indicators, and cross-supplier scan capability. See the STRATADAR agent card for full detail.

Promote to Field Intelligence

Any watched position can be promoted directly to FI with one click. The promote action navigates to Field Intelligence with the supplier and company name pre-filled via router state — ready to run research immediately. The position is removed from Active Watch on promote.

Output Engine
Module 4 — Proposal and brief generation at CI thresholds

The Output Engine generates graduated sales documents from a Relationship Profile when the CI is high enough. As of the current build, output is generated inline within Field Intelligence — no separate navigation required. The Output Engine backend remains available as a standalone module at /output for future use cases (batch generation, integration with other tools). All outputs are appended with Jason's SSI contact footer.

Endpoint: POST /api/output/generate — accepts profile_id, automatically selects the correct path based on stored CI, returns structured JSON with email + path-specific documents.

Output Paths

Path A — CONVERGENCE PROPOSAL (CI ≥ 90)
Full proposal. Supplier capability matched to named prospect need. Decision maker addressed. Specific project referenced. All buying signals incorporated.
Path B — MUTUAL VALUE BRIEF (CI ≥ 75)
Two-page value brief. Establishes shared interest without a hard pitch. Opens the conversation. Good for prospects where full intelligence is partial.
Path C — FIRST SIGNAL (CI ≥ 60)
Short introductory message. Establishes presence. No detailed capability claim. References one specific thing found in research to prove relevance.
PARK (CI < 60)
No output generated. Auto-parked to Active Watch. Honest Gate message shown. Three better alternatives suggested.

Agent Team
Supreme sales, marketing, research and promotion partners

The STRATAGENT agent team is designed around a single principle: capability-first intelligence. The question is never "what do I know about this company" — it is "given everything I can do, what does the world need that I haven't found yet." Every KB depth point added to a supplier expands the surface area the agents can match against. The richer the capability map, the more precise and surprising the opportunities they surface.

Agents operate in two orientations: reactive (you ask, they research) and proactive (they monitor, discover, and surface without being asked). The long-term vision is a fully proactive team — opportunities arrive without Jason naming them.

Geography parameter is a first-class control on all discovery agents. Rather than searching globally and generating unmanageable data volume, each hunt is scoped to a geography zone: Denmark → Scandinavia → Europe → Global. Start narrow, expand when the pipeline warrants it.

STRATEGIST
✅ BUILT
Cross-pipeline AI advisor. Available at /strategist. Reads all modules in a single pass — KB depths, CI scores, Active Watch positions, Prospect Pool candidates, Outcome Memory — and answers three questions: Who should I call? · What changed? · Are we on target?

Two modes:
Pipeline Snapshot — instant, no AI call. Shows live counts: KBs, active prospects, watched positions, outcome records. Fast status check.
Generate Brief — full Gemini call. Aggregates all data and returns a structured strategic brief.

Brief structure:
  • Week Headline — one sentence on the state of the pipeline
  • Pipeline Score (0–100) — weighted health score across all active prospects + KB depth, with reasoning
  • Top 3 Actions — highest-leverage moves this week, each with module badge (KB / FI / WATCH / SCOUT / OUTPUT), effort rating (HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW), and a Go button that navigates directly to that module
  • Who to Call — ranked prospect list with CI, urgency, and a crafted opening line for each
  • What Changed — delta from last week: new profiles, CI movements, triggered watches
  • Watch Alerts — positions STRATADAR has flagged as surfaced
  • KB Health — strongest and weakest KBs, and which to fix first to unlock the most pipeline
Files: backend/agents/strategist_agent.py · backend/routers/strategist.py · frontend/src/pages/Strategist.tsx
Endpoints: GET /api/strategist/pipeline-snapshot · POST /api/strategist/brief

Future enhancement: When STRATAGORA is feeding sector-level signals, STRATEGIST upgrades from weekly summary to a continuous strategic advisor — surfacing category-level opportunities opening over the next quarter, and directing KB enrichment effort toward the suppliers whose depth increase would unlock the most pipeline.

STRATFORGE

STRATASCOUT — Design Detail
Proactive prospect hunter — geography-scoped, capability-matched

Geography Zones

Geography is a mandatory parameter on every hunt. It limits data volume and keeps the pipeline manageable. The principle: start narrow, expand when the pipeline warrants it.

ZoneScopeWhen to use
🇩🇰 DenmarkDenmark onlyFirst hunts, testing a new supplier capability, very targeted campaigns
🌍 ScandinaviaDK, SE, NO, FIDefault starting zone for most supplier hunts
🌍 Northern EuropeScandinavia + DE, NL, BE, UK, IEWhen Scandinavian pool is saturated or supplier has broader reach
🌍 EuropeFull EU + UK, NO, CHHigh-value suppliers with pan-European capability and certification
🌐 GlobalNo restrictionOnly for MissBlue private label, STRATATIV3D retail plays, or explicit global campaigns

Hunt Logic

Each hunt run takes: selected supplier KB + geography zone + optional sector focus. Gemini (with grounding) searches within those constraints for companies that:

CriterionWhy it matters
Operational need matching KB capabilityCore relevance — the company must plausibly need what the supplier provides
Active buying signal presentNot just a potential buyer — a buyer in motion right now
Accessible decision makerNo point finding a company if there's no door to knock on
Within geography zoneData volume control + realistic sales reach
Recent signal only (current year or prior year)Old announcements are not actionable. Every signal must be dated within the last 18 months.

Signal Sources Searched

STRATASCOUT searches across two tiers of sources:

Formal sources (high confidence)
Published tenders and procurement notices · CapEx and investment announcements · Leadership appointment news · Regulatory compliance deadlines · Strategic press releases
Social & community sources (early-stage, high value)
Reddit threads where operators describe problems the supplier solves · X (Twitter) posts from sector operators describing frustrations · Blog posts and industry articles naming pain points · Review sites where customers flag gaps · LinkedIn posts from procurement or operations heads. These are the earliest signals — pain before procurement intent has formed. A Reddit thread of 50 Airbnb hosts with the same complaint is a market signal, not just one lead.
Sector Focus is a hard constraint, not a hint. "Airbnb Superhost operators" restricts the entire hunt to multi-property STR hosts and property management companies. STRATASCOUT will return fewer candidates rather than pad with off-sector results.

VALUE BRIEF READYKB status when depth is 50–79. Can run Field Intelligence. Can generate First Signal and Value Brief.
PROPOSAL READYKB status when depth is 80–89. Full proposal capability unlocked.
SINGULARITY READYKB status when depth is 90+. Peak intelligence — highest-quality proposals and convergence scoring.
STRATALYSTBuilt agent. Enriches KBs by finding and ingesting web sources. Triggered per-supplier from the KB view.
STRATASCOUTProactive prospect hunter. Geography-scoped, capability-matched. Deposits candidates to Prospect Pool for review before full FI runs.
Prospect PoolFirestore collection (prospect_pool) where STRATASCOUT deposits discovered candidates. Status: new → reviewed → promoted (FI) or dismissed.
Geography ZoneMandatory STRATASCOUT parameter controlling hunt scope: Denmark → Scandinavia → Northern Europe → Europe → Global. Start narrow.
STRATAGORAPlanned market intelligence layer. Monitors regulatory pipeline, industry trends, technology shifts. Feeds all other agents — the weather system they operate inside.
STRATFORGEPlanned product/service ideation agent. Matches supplier capabilities against market signals to generate product briefs for evaluation. Finds the next Ungunk.
STRATMESHPlanned relationship and network intelligence agent. Tracks decision maker career moves, warm entry points, industry connections across the prospect landscape.
Capability-first intelligenceCore design principle. Start from "given what I can do, what does the world need?" — not "what do I know about this prospect?" Inverts the reactive CRM model.
Manual SeedFour plain-language fields Jason fills in per KB: product_plain, buyer_type, use_case, not_this. Fed to every agent before AI reasoning. Prevents product name misinterpretation (e.g. "filter" → water treatment). Shown as "Agent Definition" panel in KB view — amber warning if not set.
STRATALINKBuilt agent. Affiliate intelligence and revenue development. Three modules: Partner Library (manage affiliate programs), Research Programs (STRATALINK finds and evaluates programs), Revenue Log (manual referral tracking with commission recording).
Adjacent OpportunitiesWhen FI researches a prospect, STRATALINK also checks the affiliate partner library for adjacent needs the prospect has. Shown as purple panel in FI results. Only appears if active partners exist in the library.
Affiliate Partner LibraryFirestore collection affiliate_partners. Stores affiliate programs Jason has joined or is evaluating. Fields: category, commission type/rate, network, referral URL, quality rating, program status.
Commission typeone-time (single payment on conversion), recurring (ongoing % of subscription), hybrid (both). Shown with symbols: 1×, ↻, 1×+↻
Referral logFirestore collection affiliate_referrals. Manual tracking: referred → in_progress → converted (commission logged) or lost. Revenue summary card shows totals.
SOCIAL_SIGNALBuying signal type from Reddit, X, blogs, or review sites where operators describe the problem the supplier solves. Earliest-stage signal — pain before procurement intent has formed. Purple badge in UI.
Hunt timeoutSTRATASCOUT hunts have a 120-second backend timeout (asyncio.wait_for). Frontend shows red ■ Stop button (AbortController) during loading. Stopped hunts deposit whatever was found before cancellation.

Gemini groundingGemini's web search capability used in research_agent.py and extraction_agent.py. Called via generate_with_grounding().
Folder SyncSystem that ingests local supplier files (PDFs, images) into the corresponding KB by matching folder names.
Brand LayerThe KB profile for a supplier that represents Jason's own product line (e.g. STRATATIV3D). Covers manufacturing capability, positioning, and buyer profiles for the brand as a whole — not individual products.
Product LayerIndividual products within a multi-product supplier, registered in the Product Library (product images) with product_name, brand, and tags. Currently the mechanism for Ungunk within STRATATIV3D.
Product Sub-KBPlanned feature. Each product in a multi-product line gets its own KB with its own Intelligence Depth score. STRATATIV3D becomes the parent brand; Ungunk and future products become child KBs.
Private LabelResellers who sell MissBlue filters under their own brand name. Tracked in the distribution_channels KB element.
